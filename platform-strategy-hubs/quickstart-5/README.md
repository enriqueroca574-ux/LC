# PrestaShop Migration Hub

PrestaShop is often selected for its flexibility and structured catalog model, especially when product variations are expressed through attributes and combinations. Migration planning typically focuses on preserving the buying model and ensuring that variation behavior remains clear to shoppers.

### PrestaShop at a glance

**Core capabilities**

* Catalog variation modeling through attributes and combinations
* Strong control over catalog structure and product variation meaning
* Support for multistore setups depending on installation scope

PrestaShop documentation states that **attributes are the basis of product variations**, which are called “combinations” in PrestaShop’s interface.

**Typical strengths**

* Good for stores that want flexible catalog management and variation control
* Strong when variation behavior is central to how customers shop

**Common complexity drivers**

* Large combination grids created by many attributes
* Store scope decisions in multistore scenarios
* SEO continuity planning and URL mapping

### What tends to matter most in PrestaShop migrations

#### Attributes and combinations drive buying behavior

Because attributes underpin combinations, migration success depends on preserving meaning: which choices are real buying choices and which are descriptive.

#### Combination-heavy products should lead your demo sample

If you validate only simple products, you can miss the risk that drives rework. Your demo sample should include the products with the most combinations and the categories that drive revenue.
